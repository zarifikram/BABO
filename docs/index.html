Abstract

Introducing the sensational Bi-axial Autonomous roBot with Obstacle Avoidance, affectionately known as BABO! Imagine a robot buddy that's not only super smart but also downright jolly. BABO is like the cool kid at the tech playground, using its magical powers of Hierarchical Q learning to master the art of crawling forward with a skip in its step, all while effortlessly sidestepping obstacles like a dance on a rainbow. Picture this: BABO is your trusty sidekick, zipping around with glee, turning learning into a thrilling adventure. With BABO, it's not just about avoiding obstacles; it's a joyous journey of discovery, making every little obstacle feel like a puzzle waiting to be solved. So, buckle up, young explorers, because BABO is here to turn the world of robotics into a fantastic, fun-filled expedition!

Frontal and Rear view of BABO. The robot is equipped with three ultrasonic sensors, one in the front and two in the back. The robot is also equipped with three servo motors to fascilitate its crawling.

Structural backbone of BABO. It is built using plexiglass, castor wheels, and L brackets.

Reward function design for forward crawl. It encourages the robot to move forward and punishes it for moving backward.

Reward function design for obstacle avoidance. It encourages the robot to steer clear of obstacles in front by sensing using two SONAR sensors.

Dataflow diagram of BABO. The scarsity of memory in the NANO is mitigated by using another NANO for data logging.

Power diagram of BABO. The power is supplied by a 7.7V battery and is regulated using a step down transformer to suit the voltage requirements for servo motors.
